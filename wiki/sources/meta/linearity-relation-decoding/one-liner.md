**Hernandez et al.** show that ~48% of tested relation types in transformers are well-approximated by **affine relational embeddings**, and that mid-layer representations are richer than final-layer ones (the "mode switch" — entities are enriched at intermediate layers, then compressed for next-token prediction). Explains why activation-communication targets intermediate layers and why simple linear projections succeed at cross-model alignment.
