A quantitative scaling framework (5 architectures × 9 LLMs × 4 benchmarks = 180 configs) showing that multi-agent scaling is **task-contingent, not monotonic** — the best variant yields +80.9% on naturally decomposable tasks but the worst degrades −70% on strictly sequential ones. The **baseline paradox** ($\beta = -0.404$): if a single agent already exceeds ~45% accuracy, coordination overhead often outweighs the gains.
