This paper, from [[cmu|CMU]], [[fair-meta|Meta AI]], and [[mbzuai|MBZUAI]], introduces **ThoughtComm**, a paradigm where LLM agents communicate by extracting and sharing **latent thoughts** — disentangled latent variables that underlie their hidden-state representations — rather than tokens, embeddings, or raw activations. The key innovation is a theoretically grounded framework that (1) formalizes agent states as generated by unknown functions of latent thoughts, (2) proves that shared thoughts, private thoughts, and the full thought-agent structure can be **identified** under minimal assumptions, and (3) implements a practical system using sparsity-regularized autoencoders and prefix adaptation.

This bridges [[latent-space-reasoning]] (intra-agent, Coconut) and [[embedding-space-communication]] (inter-agent, CIPHER) by introducing a **structured** latent communication layer — not sharing raw representations, but extracting and routing the disentangled factors that generated them.
