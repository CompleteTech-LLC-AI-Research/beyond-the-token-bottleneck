# Theoretical Foundations

The empirical results in latent reasoning and communication rest on a surprisingly small set of theoretical pillars. Understanding these foundations explains *why* continuous thoughts outperform discrete tokens, *why* cross-model communication works with simple linear maps, and *why* representations across architectures and modalities converge. Without this theory, the empirical results are a collection of tricks; with it, they form a coherent picture of what neural networks compute and how that computation can be shared.
